Võ Nguyên Giáp

The man who won the world superpowers

General Giap at his 80s

Võ Nguyên Giáp (25 August 1911 – 4 October 2013) was a Vietnamese general in the Vietnam People's Army and a politician. Giap is considered one of the greatest military strategists of the 20th century.[1] He first grew to prominence during World War II, where he served as the military leader of the Viet Minh resistance against the Japanese occupation of Vietnam. Giap was a principal commander in two wars: the First Indochina War (1946–54) and the Vietnam War (1960–75)

Here is his basic time line

  • 1911 Born in Quang Binh Province, French Indochina (Vietnam)
  • 1933 to 1938 Study at Vietnam National University of Hanoi
  • 3 May 1940 Left Hanoi and crossed the border into China. In China, Giap joined up with Hồ Chí Minh, then an adviser to the People's Liberation Army.
  • May 1941 Giap was made responsible for establishing an intelligence network and organising political bases in the far north of the country.
  • Sep 1944 The formation of the Vietnam Liberation army was proclaimed, with Giap as its commander.
  • 28 Aug 194 Giap led his men into Hanoi, and on 2 September Ho Chi Minh declared the independence of the Democratic Republic of Vietnam.
  • 1946 to 1954Commander of the Viet Minh participate in the First Indochina War
  • 13 March 1954 Giap launched the Dien Bien Phu offensive
  • Late 1950s Giap served as Minister of Defence, Commander in Chief of the People's Army of Vietnam, Deputy Prime Minister, and deputy chairman of the Defence Council.
  • 1955 to 1975 The Vietnam War
  • 1972 Launched the Easter Offensive
  • 1973 US forces retreat from Vietnam in humiliation
  • 30 Apr 1975 South Vietnam surrendered ended the Vietnam war
  • July 1976 Giáp maintained his position as Minister of National Defense and he was made Deputy Prime Minister
  • Dec 1978 He oversaw the successful Vietnamese invasion of Cambodia which drove the Khmer Rouge from power and ended the Cambodian genocide.
  • Jan 1979 Cambodia's ally China responded by invading the Cao Bang province of Vietnam in January 1979 and once again Giap was in overall responsibility for the response, which drove the Chinese out after a few months.
  • 1981 Retired from his post at the Defense Ministry
  • 1982 Retired from the Poliburo
  • 1991 Retired from the Central Committee and Deputy Prime Minister
  • 4 Oct 2013 Dies at the age of 102
